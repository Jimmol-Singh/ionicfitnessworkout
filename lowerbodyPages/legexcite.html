1. Classic Squat

The squat is the best exercise because it uses 256 muscles in one motion. Every workout plan should include the squat because it is a fundamental compound exercise used by everyone from bodybuilders to powerlifters to competitive athletes.

It is regarded by powerlifters as one of the "big three" lifts, together with the squat, deadlift, and bench press. An athlete's ability to squat explosively is a good predictor of their effectiveness on the field or court. The squat is a complex exercise for bodybuilders that works almost all of the muscles in your lower body and core.

2. Bulgarian Squat(Left)

The back leg of the Bulgarian split squat is raised on a bench or a strong chair in place of the front leg. The exercise, a unilateral, single-leg squat, places a greater emphasis on the quadriceps than other comparable lower-body compound motions.

Additionally, it calls for a lot of balance and coordination, which makes the use of the core and upper body to maintain appropriate form more difficult.

The most important thing to keep in mind when performing the Bulgarian split squat is that finding the right foot location to perform the exercise comfortably requires some trial and error.

3. Bulgarian Squat(Right)

In place of the front leg, the back leg of the Bulgarian split squat is lifted on a bench or a sturdy chair. The quadriceps are worked more during the exercise, which is a unilateral, single-leg squat, than with similar lower-body compound motions.

It also requires a lot of balance and coordination, making it more challenging to maintain proper form with the usage of the core and upper body.

The most crucial thing to remember when executing the Bulgarian split squat is that it may take some trial and error to establish the proper foot placement. core. Thanks to that position, you'll also bring your shoulders and mid-back into the equation.

6. Wall Sit

The wall sit exercise works the muscles on the front of your thighs, which is a great quad burner. This exercise is typically done to strengthen the quadriceps muscle group, the glutes, and the calves isometrically.Â 

Although the wall sit is a simple exercise, many individuals perform it incorrectly. If your hips and knees are at a straight angle (90 degrees), your back is flat against the wall, and your heels are on the floor, you are doing the wall sit correctly. The quad region should begin to feel slightly pulled. This exercise can be incorporated into any lower body workout.

1. Jumping Jack

All of your body's major muscles can be stimulated by performing jumping jacks. It is a full-body exercise that is used for warm-up and cardio exercises. Jumping jacks are a plyometric workout because they combine resistance and aerobic conditioning.

Because of its simple movements that don't need a lot of effort, it is equally suited for beginners and fitness enthusiasts. Jumping jack variations include a number of low-impact and high-impact exercises.

2. In n Out

The in-and-out exercise is a calisthenics movement that strengthens your muscles by using your own bodyweight. Start the in and out exercise by standing up straight with your feet shoulder-width apart. Lower yourself into a squat position by bending your knees and hips. Jump back into a high plank posture with your upper and lower bodies making a straight line. Place your hands on the floor in front of your feet. 4. High Knee

Although high knees appear to be a straightforward workout, performing several sets of this high-intensity motion causes your heart to race, your lower body and core muscles to contract, and you quickly begin to perspire. High knees can also be used as a warm-up, a cardio break in between resistance training activities, or a component of an HIT program.

5. Mountain Climber

Most people would find climbing a mountain to be a difficult workout, but what if the mountain were the floor? The idea behind mountain climbers is that. You'll start in a plank posture and alternately bring one knee to your chest before bringing it back out again, accelerating each time until you're "racing" against the floor.

Although the movement appears straightforward, mountain climbers use practically the entire body and increase heart rate. Mountain climbers are a simple exercise to incorporate into your morning workout, whether you do them at home, in the gym, while traveling, or even in the office break room.

1. Jumping Burpees

Get a great workout in no time with this Jumping Burpee! Stand with feet at shoulder-width. Keep your back straight as you squat down and place your hands on the floor. Brace your weight and jump back into a pushup position. Keep your core tight as you hold the pushup, drop your belly to the floor or perform an actual pushup, then jump your feet to your hands and stand back up. Immediately jump up as high as you can with your hands overhead and hips extended..

Take your jumps to the next level with this burpee series. With little more than a jump and some momentum, you'll work every area of your body with energy-efficient exercises like burpees and jump squats. Increase your endurance and build strength as you build speed by adding a finishing pushup after each rep.

This exercise is similar to a burpee but will improve balance, build your lower-body strength and endurance, promote flexibility in the hips and knees, and help you develop speed.

JUMPING BURPEES will challenge your core, cardio, and shoulder strength all at once. Get ready to feel stronger, leaner and more ready than ever before.

2. Classic Pushup

Pushups are a simple and effective bodyweight movement that can help increase strength in your upper body and core. You don't need any equipment to get started with pushups. They're suitable for beginners and individuals who are more advanced with exercise

Do you want to build your upper body with exercises that are simple and effective? We think pushups are the perfect fit for you!

Pushups are a great way to improve strength and cardiovascular fitness. If you’re looking for something that’s easy to do with little equipment, this exercise is perfect for you. It also works your core muscles, as well as the shoulders, chest and arms. Beginners should start slowly and build up over time.

The pushup is a great exercise to integrate into your workout routine. Pushups work your chest, shoulders and arms. By always keeping your core tight, you will reap the benefits of a strong upper body and an increased sense of strength and control.

3. Mountain Climbers

The mountain climber is typically considered an exercise to target your ab muscles. That's because you set up in a high plank (pushup) position, which challenges you to brace your core. Thanks to that position, you'll also bring your shoulders and mid-back into the equation.

Set up in a high plank (pushup) position, which challenges you to brace your core. Thanks to that position, you'll also bring your shoulders and mid-back into the equation. Once you get moving, your legs and hips are also active.

The high plank position of the mountain climber challenge your core and shoulders, while also training your legs and hips. It’s a great workout for all-around fitness.

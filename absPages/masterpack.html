1. Crossover Crunch(left)

The internal and external obliques are the target of the crossover crunch. These are the abdominal muscles on either side. They help with actions like moving your upper body while keeping your lower body still, or twisting your trunk from side to side.

When playing their preferred sport, certain athletes benefit from having powerful obliques. For instance, baseball players rely on maximum trunk rotation while pitching and hitting. 2 Sprint times can also be increased with the use of an obliques-building exercise regimen.

Having a strong core boosts your general health and strength, which is more than just your appearance for non-athletes. Having a strong core can help with posture, injury prevention, and more.

3. Scissors

Scissor kicks are a technique that strengthens the abs and core. According to Arteaga, they are carried out by crossing the legs and moving them out and in horizontally. DiGiorgio continues, "You perform scissor kicks while lying face up, your legs straight and raised roughly 45 degrees off the ground. As you lift one leg, you lower the other toward the floor, creating a scissor motion as you gently alternate the two legs."

4. Elbow Plank

A common exercise for strengthening the abs and core muscles is the plank. You could have pondered whether variation is more efficient after observing people perform them on their hands and elbows.

Do planks work better on the hands or the elbows? For a more effective core workout, perform planks on your elbows. Your body is more horizontal to the floor when you perform planks on your elbows, which makes your core muscles work harder to stabilize you.

However, for those who are new to exercise, have very weak core muscles, or wish to lessen pressure on the lower back, planks on the hands are preferable.
